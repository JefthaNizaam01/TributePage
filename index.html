Nelson Mandela

Long Walk to Freedom

"True courage requires not only strength of will but strength of belief. What motivated Nelson Mandela and drove him to risk his life for freedom was a burning passion that, irrespective of colour, race and background, all people are created equal and his list of historic achievements starts with a multiracial South Africa."

Nelson Mandela was a South African anti-apartheid revolutionary, politician, and philanthropist who served as President of South Africa from 1994 to 1999. He was the country's first black head of state and the first elected in a fully representative democratic election. Mandela's lifelong dedication to ending apartheid and promoting reconciliation earned him global acclaim as a symbol of freedom and equality.

Life and Work

  • 1918- Nelson Mandela was born on July 18, 1918, in the village of Mvezo in Umtata, then part of South Africa.
  • 1943 -Mandela joined the African National Congress (ANC) and became actively involved in the anti-apartheid movement.
  • 1962 - Mandela was arrested and convicted of sabotage and other charges. He was sentenced to life imprisonment and spent 27 years in prison.
  • 1990 - On February 11, 1990, President F.W. de Klerk announced Mandela's release from prison, signaling the end of apartheid. Mandela's release marked a crucial turning point in South African history.
  • 1993 - Mandela was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize, along with F.W. de Klerk, for their efforts to dismantle the apartheid system peacefully.
  • 1994 - South Africa held its first democratic elections in April, and Nelson Mandela became the country's first black president. His presidency focused on reconciliation between the previously divided racial communities.
  • 1995 - Mandela played a key role in the Rugby World Cup hosted by South Africa. His support for the South African rugby team, the Springboks, helped unite the nation and bridge racial divides, as depicted in the movie "Invictus."
  • 1999 - Mandela stepped down as president, having served one term. He continued to be actively involved in various humanitarian causes and global peace initiatives.
  • 2004 - Mandela announced his retirement from public life, though he remained a global symbol of resistance to oppression and a champion for peace and reconciliation.
  • 2013 - Nelson Mandela passed away on December 5, 2013, at the age of 95. His death was mourned globally, and he is remembered as a symbol of the struggle for freedom, justice, and equality.
